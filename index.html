About Me

I have a degree in Law and Economics from Pompeu Fabra University, and a Master in Law (LLM) and Juris Science Doctor (PhD) from Cornell Law School (NY, USA). I am a lawyer in the state of New York and in Barcelona. I was law clerk to Hon. Edwin H. Stern, Presiding Judge of the New Jersey Appellate Division. I am currently a Ramon y Cajal researcher at the Faculty of Law at Pompeu Fabra University.

My research is in law, especially private law from an economic perspective, focusing on contract law, consumption, and the regulation and interaction of private agents in markets â€“ with special attention to digital markets.

I am coordinator of the European project Justice, Fundamental Rights and Artificial Intelligence (JuLIA) funded by DG Justice, which aims to train judges on the impact of artificial intelligence on fundamental rights.

I am also principal investigator of the projects AlgorithmLaw, Algorithmic Decisions and the Law: Opening the Black Box, which studies the functioning of algorithms in order to reflect their technical heterogeneity in legal norms and protect fundamental rights, and iConsumers, which studies the functioning of algorithms in digital markets and their impact on consumer rights. These two projects are funded by the Spanish Research Agency.

I have been a visiting professor at Brooklyn Law School, at the Wirtschaftsrecht Institute of the University of Kassel, at the Department of Economics and Law at Montenegro University, and at the Faculty of Law at the University of Trento. I have been a visiting researcher at the Center for South African studies at the University of Western Cape and at New York University (NYU).

Do not hesitate to contact me if you want to get in touch!
